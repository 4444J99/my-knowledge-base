<div>E. Nested Structure (Inception, Cloud Atlas, Rashomon)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Best for: Complex, nonlinear storytelling.</div>
<div>  •  A story within a story, or multiple intersecting timelines.</div>
<div>  •  The structure mirrors itself (e.g., characters influencing events across different layers).</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>4. Additional Writing Activities</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>To help him play with structure, here are a few focused exercises:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>A. Rearranging Narrative Order</div>
<div>  •  Take a story idea and write three versions:</div>
<div>  1.  Linear version (Beginning → Middle → End)</div>
<div>  2.  Reverse version (End → Middle → Beginning)</div>
<div>  3.  Fragmented version (Reveal events out of order)</div>
<div>  •  This teaches how structure shapes tension and emotion.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>B. Writing a Scene with Constraints</div>
<div>  •  Write the same scene four times, but each time:</div>
<div>  1.  Change the POV (First-person, third-person, omniscient).</div>
<div>  2.  Change the tense (Past, present, future).</div>
