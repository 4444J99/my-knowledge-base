## Content
<div>It's important to talk about the common grammar, punctuation, and spelling mistakes in college writing. One of the most common mistakes is to use incorrect verb tenses. Many students mix past, present, and future tense when writing their essays or assignments.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div> Example: Then vs. Than</div>
<div>Wrong: That presentation was better then the first one.</div>
<div>Right: That presentation was better than the first one.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Why? You use then when you’re talking about time (e.g., “I’m going to go to the meeting, then to lunch”), and use than when you’re comparing things (e.g., “I’m older than he is”).</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Another mistake is misusing apostrophes. Students mistakenly use apostrophes in plural nouns, which should not be the case. The use of apostrophes shows possession or indicates contractions.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div> Example: Its vs. It's</div>
<div>Wrong: The company just celebrated it’s eighth year since it went public.</div>
<div>Right: The company just celebrated its eighth year since it went public.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Why? You use it's is short-had for &quotit is&quot (e.g., “It's dinner time!&quot), and use 'its' for possession, like his or hers (e.g., “The dog chased its tail.”).</div>
