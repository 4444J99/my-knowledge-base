## Content
<div>Asking questions can help you both identify a topic for writing and identify ideas about that topic. For example, you may want to ask and answer questions such as &quotWhat am I interested in?&quot or &quotWhat news article/issue has captured my interest recently?&quot to identify a writing topic.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Brainstorming</div>
<div>Brainstorming aims to develop ideas once you already have a topic. When you brainstorm, you freely associate to develop all possible ideas related to a topic. You jot down any ideas, no matter how different they are, so that you can capture all the parts of the case that occur to you.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Graphic Organizers</div>
<div>Graphic organizers aim to develop ideas about a topic and group similar ideas. One good example is a cognitive map or bubble map.Links to an external site. Jot down your thoughts and, as you are doing so, link related information to show relationships.</div>
