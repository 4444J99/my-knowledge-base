## Content
<div>Writing Guide for Derek: Structure, Exploration, and Momentum</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>This guide is designed to help you break through creative blocks, explore story ideas, and refine narrative structure. It’s broken into theory sections followed by practical exercises so you can immediately apply what you learn.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Introduction: Embracing the Exploratory Phase</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Before diving into structure, let go of the idea that your first attempts need to be good. Writing isn’t about producing a perfect draft—it’s about discovery. The first stage is exploratory writing, also called vomit drafting:</div>
<div>  •  No one will see this but you.</div>
<div>  •  Your job is just to get ideas down.</div>
<div>  •  You don’t need to start at the beginning—write whatever comes to mind.</div>
<div>  •  This phase includes notes, sketches, and brainstorming—not just prose.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>  Activity: 10-Minute Vomit Draft</div>
<div>    •  Set a timer for 10 minutes.</div>
<div>  •  Write anything related to your story (character backstory, a scene idea, a theme).</div>
<div>  •  Don’t stop. Don’t edit. Just write through the mess.</div>
