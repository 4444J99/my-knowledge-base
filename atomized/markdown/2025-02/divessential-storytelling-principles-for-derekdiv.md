<div>Essential Storytelling Principles for Derek</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>1. The Core of a Narrative: Change Through Antagonism</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>A story is about a protagonist undergoing change due to an antagonistic force rising in their life.</div>
<div>  •  A character must be making decisions. Every scene should involve a choice, and that choice pushes the story forward.</div>
<div>  •  Conflict isn’t just fights—it’s resistance. This could be internal (doubt, fear) or external (a rival, an oppressive system, the weather).</div>
<div>  •  Antagonism isn’t just “the villain.” It’s anything that forces the protagonist to struggle and grow.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Activity: Find the Antagonist</div>
<div>  1.  Choose a character.</div>
<div>  2.  Identify what opposes them—what’s stopping them from getting what they want?</div>
<div>  3.  Ask: How does this force escalate? How does the protagonist adapt?</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>2. The Positive and Negative Pulse</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Every moment in a story should have a pulse—a shift in energy that moves the narrative.</div>
<div>  •  Scenes aren’t just things happening; they shift in meaning.</div>
<div>  •  A scene starts in one state and ends in another. (Positive → Negative, Negative → Positive)</div>
