<div><tt>Symbolism in Eudora Welty’s &quotA Worn Path&quot: Perseverance and Sacrifice</tt></div>
<div><tt><br></tt></div>
<div><tt>Eudora Welty’s short story A Worn Path uses symbolism to show the themes of perseverance and sacrifice. Phoenix Jackson, an elderly African American woman, goes on a tough journey to get medicine for her sick grandson, facing many challenges along the way. Two important symbols—the worn path itself and the thorn bush—help show her strength and how much she is willing to sacrifice.</tt></div>
<div><tt><br></tt></div>
<div><tt>The worn path represents Phoenix’s difficult life and her strong commitment to helping her grandson. The path is long and full of obstacles, but she walks it over and over because she loves him. Her journey is both real and symbolic, showing the struggles of aging, poverty, and racial inequality. Even with these difficulties, she keeps going. Welty describes, “She walked slowly in the dark pine shadows, moving a little from side to side in her steps, with the balanced heaviness and lightness of a pendulum in a grandfather clock” (Welty, 1941). This description shows how her journey never really ends, just like the hardships she faces in life.</tt></div>
