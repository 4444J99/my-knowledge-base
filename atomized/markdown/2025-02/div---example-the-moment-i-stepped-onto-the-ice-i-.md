## Content
<div>  - Example: *“The moment I stepped onto the ice, I knew I had made a mistake.”*  </div>
<div>- **Thesis Statement:** Establishes the main lesson, insight, or transformation in the story.  </div>
<div>  - Example: *“Through failure, I discovered resilience.”*  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>##### **B. The Body: Plot Development**  </div>
<div>- **Exposition:** Provides background on the characters, setting, and situation.  </div>
<div>- **Rising Action:** Introduces **conflict, tension, or obstacles** that make the story engaging.  </div>
<div>- **Climax:** The most intense, revealing, or emotional moment of the story.  </div>
<div>  - Example: *“As I sat outside the principal’s office, I realized I had to tell the truth.”*  </div>
<div>- **Falling Action:** How the character processes the experience.  </div>
<div>- **Resolution:** The takeaway or **lesson learned** from the story.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>##### **C. Conclusion: Wrapping It Up**  </div>
<div>- Reflects on the significance of the story.  </div>
<div>- Leaves a **lasting impression** on the reader.  </div>
<div>  - Example: *“Even though I lost that game, I gained something more valuable—a sense of perseverance.”*  </div>
