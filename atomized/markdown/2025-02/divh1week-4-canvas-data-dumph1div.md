<div><h2>Week 4 | Overview</h2></div>
<div>Compare and Contrast</div>
<div>This week, our focus sharpens on the compare-and-contrast essay. This is an approach to writing where we consider the similarities and/or differences between various subjects. It's like holding a magnifying glass to two things and examining what makes them alike and/or separates them. To illustrate, think about how you approach purchasing a car or a new cell phone. You scrutinize both the shared features and the contrasting aspects before deciding. It's a valuable mindset to apply to your writing.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>As you write your compare and contrast ideas for this week's initial discussion post, I encourage you to consider the things you've purchased or aspects of your life that lend themselves well to comparison and contrast. It could be anything, from the different hobbies you enjoy to the contrasting personalities of your friends. It could even include the diverse cuisines you've tried. The options are limitless, so let your imagination run wild!</div>
