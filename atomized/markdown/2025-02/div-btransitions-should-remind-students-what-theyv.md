<div>✅ <b>Transitions should remind students what they’ve already learned and prepare them for what’s coming next.</b><br></div>
<div>✅ <b>Key words &amp phrases should repeat between sections to recontextualize ideas.</b><br></div>
<div>✅ <b>No section stands alone—each builds into the next, both within the day and across weeks.</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>📖<b> 8. Discussion &amp Question Formatting</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>✅ <b>Each discussion must include:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  <b>Concrete Questions (Observation-Based)</b> (<i>Fact-based, directly from the text.</i>).</div>
<div>  •  <b>Abstract Questions (Concept-Based)</b> (<i>Why does this matter? What broader themes or ideas emerge?</i>).</div>
<div>  •  <b>Connecting Questions (Application-Based)</b> (<i>How does this text relate to other works, real life, or modern media?</i>).</div>
<div>  •  <b>Exploratory Questions (Challenging &amp Speculative)</b> (<i>What if the story had unfolded differently? How might another writer approach this same concept?</i>).</div>
<div><br></div>
