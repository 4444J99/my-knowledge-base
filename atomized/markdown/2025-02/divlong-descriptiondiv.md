<div><h2>Week 4 | Organize Compare and Contrast Essay Point-by-Point</h2></div>
<div>Organize Compare and Contrast Essay Point-by-Point</div>
<div>There are two main ways to organize a compare-and-contrast essay. The first is by comparing the subjects point by point.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>In this option for compare-and-contrast essay writing, you take each point one at a time. Start with the first point, then move on, and make comparisons for the second. Repeat this process until you've reached the end of your essay. In this structure, it's important to remember that your goal is to emphasize the similarities and differences between the two subjects; don't let one side or the other dominate your paper.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>There are a few different ways you can format this type of essay. One common method is to have one paragraph dedicated to each point you're comparing, with a topic sentence introducing each mini-topic. Another approach is to do all your talking about one subject before moving on to the next; in this case, be sure to indicate which subject you're discussing in each paragraph. Either way, keep your readers engaged by using strong transition words and phrases as you move from point to point.</div>
