## Content
<div>  2.  Change the setting – Place it in a wildly different environment.</div>
<div>  3.  Change the protagonist – What if they were younger, older, from a different background?</div>
<div>  4.  Reverse the genre – Turn a drama into a thriller, a tragedy into a comedy.</div>
<div>  5.  Flip the conflict – What if the antagonist was the protagonist?</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>SECTION 3: STRUCTURING YOUR STORY (Multiple Approaches)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Once you have your idea, it’s time to choose a structure that best fits your story.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Option 1: Three-Act Structure</div>
<div>  •  Act 1: Introduce protagonist, world, and inciting incident.</div>
<div>  •  Act 2: Conflict escalates, protagonist struggles, major turning point.</div>
<div>  •  Act 3: Climax and resolution.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Option 2: The Hero’s Journey</div>
<div>  •  Ordinary World → Call to Adventure → Refusal → Mentor Appears → Crossing the Threshold → Challenges and Growth → Ordeal → Transformation → Return with Knowledge.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Option 3: Kishōtenketsu (Japanese Structure)</div>
<div>  •  Introduction (Ki): Establish world and characters.</div>
<div>  •  Development (Shō): Deepen understanding of the situation.</div>
