<div><h2>Week 4 | Compare and Contrast Essay</h2></div>
<div>Compare and Contrast Essays</div>
<div>A compare-and-contrast essay is a type of academic writing. In this essay type, a writer selects two subjects (objects, people, or places) and examines their similarities and differences. These lectures will guide you through the components. They will structure the essentials for crafting a compare-and-contrast essay, whether you have been writing for a while or are just beginning to write.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Long Description</div>
<div><br></div>
<div> </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>There are two main ways to organize a compare-and-contrast essay: </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Subject-by-subject: In this organization, you first discuss all the similarities between the two subjects. Then, discuss all the differences between the two subjects.</div>
<div>Point-by-point: In this organization, you alternate between discussing the similarities and differences between the two subjects. </div>
<div>No matter which one of the two organizational patterns you choose, your essay should include an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion.</div>
