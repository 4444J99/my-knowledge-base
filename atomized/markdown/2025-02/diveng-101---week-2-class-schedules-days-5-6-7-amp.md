<div>## **Week 2, Day 5 Class Schedule**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>### **I. Writing Warm-Up**  </div>
<div>#### **1. Purpose**  </div>
<div>- A daily exercise to ease into writing, develop fluency, and strengthen storytelling skills.  </div>
<div>- Encourages students to explore personal experiences and **develop descriptive writing techniques**.  </div>
<div>- Helps students craft compelling narratives by **practicing voice, detail, and emotional depth**.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>#### **2. Prompt Options**  </div>
<div>- **Concrete:** Think about a time when you were young and convinced of something—an idea, a belief, or a memory. How did you come to this conviction, and did it ever change?  </div>
<div>- **Abstract:** How do our memories shape the stories we tell? Can a memory ever be completely accurate?  </div>
<div>- **Connecting:** Compare two different ways you might tell the same personal story—once to a friend and once in a formal written essay. How would the storytelling change?  </div>
<div>- **Exploratory:** If you had to write a one-sentence summary of the most important lesson you’ve learned in life so far, what would it be and why?  </div>
