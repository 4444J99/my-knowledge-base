## Content
<div>  •  Not good vs. evil. It’s momentum—how things get better or worse.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Example of Scene Pulses</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Scene Start  Action  Scene End</div>
<div>Hopeful  Protagonist receives bad news  Desperate</div>
<div>Angry  Argument turns into reconciliation  Relieved</div>
<div>Confused  Discovers a clue  Determined</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Activity: Scene Pulse Mapping</div>
<div>  1.  Write a simple scene.</div>
<div>  2.  Mark what emotional state it starts in.</div>
<div>  3.  Mark how it changes by the end.</div>
<div>  4.  Rewrite it with a clearer shift in pulse.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>3. Macro &amp Micro Dramatic Structure (Story as a Graph)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>A narrative is like a waveform—it goes up and down with positive and negative shifts over time.</div>
<div>  •  Big Picture (Macro Pulse): The whole story has highs and lows.</div>
<div>  •  Small Picture (Micro Pulse): Each scene also has highs and lows.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Think of it like this:</div>
<div>  •  One full story = multiple waves of rising and falling action.</div>
<div>  •  Each scene = a mini-wave that contributes to the bigger picture.</div>
