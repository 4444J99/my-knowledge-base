<div><h2>Week 6 | Editing</h2></div>
<div>Editing Your Work</div>
<div>Editing goes beyond proofreading. It involves a more comprehensive evaluation of the piece's content, organization, and structure. It aims to refine the message, coherence, and logical flow of ideas. During the editing phase, writers assess the clarity of their arguments, the strength of their supporting evidence, and the effectiveness of their transitions between paragraphs and sections. They may rephrase sentences to improve clarity, reorganize paragraphs to enhance ideas' logical progression, and remove or add information to ensure the piece is well-balanced and comprehensive. Editing requires a critical eye, attention to detail, and the willingness to make substantial revisions to enhance the overall quality of the writing.</div>
