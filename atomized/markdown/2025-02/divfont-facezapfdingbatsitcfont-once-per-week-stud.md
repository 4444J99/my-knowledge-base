## Content
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> Once per week, students submit an XP Update Form (Physical or Digital).</div>
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> The form asks them:</div>
<div>  •  What challenges did you complete?</div>
<div>  •  What skill checks did you attempt?</div>
<div>  •  What XP-rewarding actions did you take?</div>
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> The professor verifies and updates the master XP sheet once a week.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><font face=".AppleColorEmojiUI">📜</font> Physical XP Tracking Board in the Classroom</div>
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> A Leaderboard displays general rankings (not exact XP values).</div>
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> Students see where they stand but don’t know exact XP numbers of others.</div>
<div><font face="ZapfDingbatsITC">✔</font> Leaderboard updates happen weekly, adding an element of suspense.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><font face=".AppleColorEmojiUI">📜</font> Why This Works:</div>
<div>  •  Students are responsible for tracking their XP—removing constant oversight.</div>
<div>  •  Simple once-a-week submission ensures XP is logged correctly.</div>
<div>  •  Leaderboard gamifies the system without overwhelming in-class time.</div>
