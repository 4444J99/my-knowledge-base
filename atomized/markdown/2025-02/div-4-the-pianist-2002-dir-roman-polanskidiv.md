## Content
<div>  4.  The Pianist (2002) – Dir. Roman Polanski</div>
<div>  •  Biographical Drama</div>
<div>  •  Themes: Survival, resilience, the relationship between suffering and art.</div>
<div>  •  Relevance: While different in setting (WWII), The Pianist shows how discipline and survival are deeply intertwined, much like Vagabond and Whiplash.</div>
<div>  5.  Amadeus (1984) – Dir. Miloš Forman</div>
<div>  •  Historical Biopic / Musical Drama</div>
<div>  •  Themes: Jealousy, the burden of genius, mentorship as rivalry.</div>
<div>  •  Relevance: Explores toxic mentorship and competition in music, akin to Whiplash and Real, but from the perspective of an embittered, less-talented rival (Salieri).</div>
<div>  6.  Rocky (1976) – Dir. John G. Avildsen</div>
<div>  •  Sports Drama</div>
<div>  •  Themes: Endurance, self-worth, mentorship, underdog narrative.</div>
<div>  •  Relevance: Rocky introduces the mentor-student bond, the struggle to prove oneself, and the grit of physical and emotional endurance, mirroring Real and Vagabond’s underdog themes.</div>
