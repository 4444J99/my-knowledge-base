<div>Forging a Narrative</div>
<div>This week, you will begin writing your narrative essay so we can look at them closer. A narrative essay is a type of essay where the writer tells a story from their personal experience. It usually involves characters, setting, plot, and dialogue. This type of essay aims to engage the reader in an interesting story while also conveying a message or lesson.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>You must choose the right topic to start your essay on the right foot. You completed this in Week 1, but it bears repeating. The topic should be something you have experienced firsthand, like a real-life memory you experienced and wish to express in narrative form. This will make it easier for you to write authentically and capture the reader's attention.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Another critical element of a successful narrative essay is creating vivid descriptions through sensory language. This allows readers to imagine themselves in the story and connect emotionally with what's happening. For example, &quotThe sun was a fiery orb casting a warm golden glow on the fields.&quot</div>
