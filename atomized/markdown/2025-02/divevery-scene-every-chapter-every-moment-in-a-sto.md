## Context
From document: Writing Guide for Derek: Structure, Exploration, and Momentum — chunk 3 (chunk 3/8)

## Content
<div>Every scene, every chapter, every moment in a story should have a pulse—a shift in emotional energy.</div>
<div>  •  Scenes aren’t just things happening; they shift in meaning.</div>
<div>  •  A scene starts in one state and ends in another. (Positive → Negative, Negative → Positive)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Scene Start  Action  Scene End</div>
<div>Hopeful  Protagonist receives bad news  Desperate</div>
<div>Angry  Argument turns into reconciliation  Relieved</div>
<div>Confused  Discovers a clue  Determined</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>  Activity: Scene Pulse Mapping</div>
<div>    1.  Write a short scene.</div>
<div>  2.  Identify the emotional state at the start.</div>
<div>  3.  Identify how the scene shifts by the end.</div>
<div>  4.  If there’s no shift, revise it to introduce a positive or negative pulse change.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>3. Macro &amp Micro Dramatic Structure (Story as a Graph)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>A story is like a wave, rising and falling with tension and resolution.</div>
<div>  •  Big Picture (Macro Pulse): The whole story has highs and lows.</div>
<div>  •  Small Picture (Micro Pulse): Each scene also has highs and lows.</div>
