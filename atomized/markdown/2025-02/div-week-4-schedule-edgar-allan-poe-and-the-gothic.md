<div>## 1. Recommendations and Peeves Opener  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>### 1.1 Purpose  </div>
<div>- Build rapport and spark engagement by sharing media recommendations and personal frustrations.  </div>
<div>- Introduce reflective and exploratory thinking to prepare for literary analysis.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>### 1.2 Structure  </div>
<div>- **Recommendations Prompt:** Share a favorite piece of media (book, movie, TV show, music, podcast, etc.) and explain why it’s meaningful to you.  </div>
<div>- **Peeves Prompt:** Share a “pet peeve”—something minor that’s been frustrating or annoying lately.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>### 1.3 Defining Pet Peeves  </div>
<div>A pet peeve is a specific thing that irritates or annoys someone disproportionately.  </div>
<div>- The term originated in the early 20th century, deriving from *peeve*, meaning an annoyance or grievance, first recorded in American English around 1911.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>### Discussion Questions  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>#### Concrete:  </div>
<div>- What is one “pet peeve” you’ve observed in a character from a story, film, or show?  </div>
