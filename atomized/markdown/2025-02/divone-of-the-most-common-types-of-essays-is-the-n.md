## Content
<div>One of the most common types of essays is the narrative essay. This type involves telling a story or personal experience. It often includes vivid descriptions, sensory details, and a clear beginning, middle, and end.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Process analysis is an essay that explains how something works or how to do something step by step. It may involve breaking down a complex process into smaller, more manageable steps for the reader to understand better.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Compare-contrast essays focus on identifying similarities and differences between two or more subjects. The writer must carefully analyze each subject's characteristics and clarify their similarities and differences in an organized manner through comparison and contrast techniques.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>The persuasive essay seeks to convince readers of a certain point of view or belief by presenting strong arguments supported by evidence. It typically uses emotional appeals, logical reasoning, and rhetorical devices to persuade readers.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div> Knowing the goal of your essay will help you write an effective essay, regardless of the type.</div>
