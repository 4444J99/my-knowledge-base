## Content
<div>4. Character Deep-Dive</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Characters are often more engaging when their history is well-developed, even if much of it doesn’t make it into the final draft.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Activity:</div>
<div>  •  Choose a character and answer:</div>
<div>  •  What was their most formative childhood experience?</div>
<div>  •  What do they regret the most?</div>
<div>  •  What’s their biggest fear?</div>
<div>  •  What’s in their pockets right now?</div>
<div>  •  Write a journal entry from their perspective at three different points in their life: childhood, a crisis moment, and old age.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>5. The McKee Conflict Triangle (Story by Robert McKee)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Robert McKee emphasizes the importance of conflict-driven storytelling. Every scene should have conflict to drive the narrative forward.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Activity:</div>
<div>  1.  Write a short scene with external conflict (protagonist vs. antagonist).</div>
<div>  2.  Write the same scene but with internal conflict (protagonist wrestling with a decision).</div>
<div>  3.  Rewrite the scene adding philosophical conflict (larger ideological struggle).</div>
