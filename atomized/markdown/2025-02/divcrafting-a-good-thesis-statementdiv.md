<div>Crafting a Good Thesis Statement</div>
<div>Once you have a topic for your essay and are all set to begin writing, crafting a powerful thesis statement is the next important step. Think of the thesis statement as the base of your argument. It lets your readers know your stance on the topic and explains why it's important. A strong thesis statement is the key to writing a solid essay. Here are some tips for writing a strong thesis statement:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Make it arguable: A good thesis statement should be debatable; it should make an argument that others might reasonably disagree with.</div>
<div>Be specific: Your thesis should be as clear as possible. A good thesis is not simply a list of facts or a topic description; it takes a position on an issue.</div>
<div>Take a stance: Your thesis should be clear on your topic and narrow enough. Avoid making statements that are too general or too specific, and avoid phrases like &quotin this essay, I will…&quot which make your paper sound more like a book report than an essay.</div>
<div>Support your claims: Your thesis should be supported by evidence from your paper—facts, examples, statistics, quotations, etc.—and must be relatable to the assignment.</div>
