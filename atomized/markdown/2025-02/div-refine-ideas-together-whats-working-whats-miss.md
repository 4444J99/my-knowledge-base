# <div>  •  Refine ideas together (what’s working, what’s missing).</div>

## Context
From document: Essential Storytelling Principles for Derek — chunk 4 (chunk 4/5)

## Content
<div>  •  Refine ideas together (what’s working, what’s missing).</div>
<div>  •  Keep momentum going without pressure.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>How This Works</div>
<div>  1.  Derek sends an email with:</div>
<div>  •  A rough idea he’s exploring.</div>
<div>  •  Any struggles he’s having (e.g., “I don’t know what happens next” or “My character feels flat”).</div>
<div>  •  A question or prompt for feedback.</div>
<div>  2.  I’ll reply with:</div>
<div>  •  Possible directions, clarifications, or questions to deepen the idea.</div>
<div>  •  A challenge (e.g., “Write this scene from a different POV”).</div>
<div>  •  Encouragement to stay in the exploratory phase—don’t overthink yet.</div>
<div>  3.  He responds with a revision or next step.</div>
<div>  4.  We repeat as needed until he feels confident in his direction.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>This keeps things low-pressure, idea-driven, and momentum-based.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Final Takeaways</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><font face=".AppleColorEmojiUI">✅</font> Stories are about change. If a character isn’t making choices, they’re stuck.</div>
<div><font face=".AppleColorEmojiUI">✅</font> Scenes must shift. Every scene should move from one state to another.</div>
