<div>📌 <b>McKee’s Principle Applied:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  If conflict <b>doesn’t escalate</b>, the audience loses interest.</div>
<div>  •  Every scene must <b>raise the stakes emotionally, physically, or psychologically.</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>5. The Ending Must Be Both Surprising and Inevitable</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>McKee argues that the best endings are:</div>
<div>  •  <b>Surprising (not predictable)</b> → They <b>don’t follow clichés or obvious setups</b>.</div>
<div>  •  <b>Inevitable (earned through story logic)</b> → Looking back, the ending feels like <b>the only possible resolution</b>.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>🔥 <b>How Whiplash Achieves This</b><br></div>
<div>  •  <b>Surprising:</b> Instead of <b>giving up or winning a competition</b>, Andrew defies Fletcher onstage, stealing control of the performance.</div>
