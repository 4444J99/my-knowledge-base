## Content
<div>Every story needs **conflict**, which drives the plot forward and keeps readers engaged. Conflicts can be:  </div>
<div>- **Internal (Man vs. Self):** The protagonist struggles with personal dilemmas or emotions.  </div>
<div>- **External (Man vs. Nature, Society, or Other Characters):** The character faces external challenges, such as societal pressure or dangerous environments.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>The **resolution** should show how the protagonist **grows or changes** because of their experiences. </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>##### **Theme &amp Moral**  </div>
<div>Every strong narrative has an underlying **theme** or **message** that connects with the reader on a deeper level. This could be a moral lesson, a universal truth, or an insight into human nature.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>#### **4. Discussion Questions:**  </div>
<div>- **Concrete:** Think of a personal story that has stuck with you. What makes it memorable?  </div>
<div>- **Abstract:** Can a story still be “true” even if small details are changed in the retelling?  </div>
<div>- **Connecting:** How do your favorite books, movies, or personal experiences shape your own storytelling?  </div>
<div>- **Exploratory:** If you were to summarize your life story in one sentence, what would it say?  </div>
