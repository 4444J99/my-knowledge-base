<div>Proofreading</div>
<div>Proofreading involves carefully reviewing the document to identify and correct grammar, punctuation, spelling, and syntax errors. It focuses on the mechanics of the writing, ensuring that the text adheres to the rules of language usage. During proofreading, one should pay attention to subject-verb agreement, proper use of punctuation marks, correct spelling of words, and consistency in formatting and style. Reading the text slowly and carefully is essential, checking for any mistakes that might have been overlooked during the writing process. By proofreading diligently, writers can eliminate errors that could distract readers and undermine the overall professionalism of their work.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>proofread: grammar, sentence structure, formatting,  punctuation, spelling, and capitalization.</div>
<div>Proofreading Tips!</div>
<div>Begin by choosing an online grammar resource you can return to again and again to acquaint yourself with the rules of grammar.</div>
<div>Websites like Purdue OWL and Grammarly are great options.</div>
<div>Check for one type of error at a time—don’t overwhelm yourself.</div>
<div>You are smarter than your word processor—don’t rely on it to correct mistakes.</div>
