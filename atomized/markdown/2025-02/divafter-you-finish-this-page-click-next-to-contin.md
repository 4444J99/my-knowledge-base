<div><h2>Week 4 | Specifics of Grammar</h2></div>
<div>Specifics of Grammar</div>
<div>We will visit three grammar topics before we end our lessons: misplaced modifiers, dangling modifiers, and run-on sentences.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>A misplaced modifier is a word, phrase, or clause that needs to be in the right place in relation to the word it describes. It is incorrectly positioned in relation to the word it describes or modifies. The result is a sentence that lacks coherence, feels clumsy, or conveys unintended meanings.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>An example might be: Running down the street, the dog chased the ball.</div>
