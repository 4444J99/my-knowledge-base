<div><h2>Week 5 | Overview</h2></div>
<div>Credible Sources and APA</div>
<div>Imagine getting home repair advice from a pet store owner or medical advice from a contractor. You would hesitate and instead seek guidance from professionals in their respective fields. This scenario highlights the crucial concept of credibility. When constructing a persuasive argument or thesis, the strength of your supporting evidence is paramount. That's why it's vital to avoid the use of first-person speech, such as &quotI,&quot &quotyou,&quot &quotme,&quot &quotmy,&quot &quotour,&quot &quotus,&quot and &quotwe.&quot Avoid personal opinions and examples based on experience. Instead, adopting a strictly third-person perspective enhances the credibility of your writing.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>This week's module will focus on distinguishing between credible and non-credible sources. You'll learn to identify reliable sources that add weight to your arguments. Meanwhile, you will be cautious of unreliable or biased information. Additionally, we'll delve into the intricacies of incorporating these sources using the widely recognized APA formatting style. Properly citing and referencing your sources showcases your attention to detail. It adds credibility to your work.</div>
