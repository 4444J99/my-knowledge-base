## Content
<div>- **First-Person Narrative:** Written from the perspective of the protagonist (e.g., &quotI walked through the empty hall...&quot). This provides an **intimate and personal** tone.  </div>
<div>- **Third-Person Narrative:** Uses &quothe/she/they&quot pronouns (e.g., &quotShe hesitated before opening the letter...&quot). This allows for **a broader viewpoint** and insight into multiple characters.  </div>
<div>- **Character Development:** A well-written character should have **motivations, emotions, and challenges**. Readers should be able to relate to or at least understand them.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>##### **Plot &amp Structure**  </div>
<div>A compelling narrative follows a **structured sequence of events**:  </div>
<div>1. **Exposition:** Introduces the setting, characters, and background information.  </div>
<div>2. **Rising Action:** Builds tension through obstacles and developments.  </div>
<div>3. **Climax:** The turning point or most intense part of the story.  </div>
<div>4. **Falling Action:** The events that unfold after the climax.  </div>
<div>5. **Resolution:** The conclusion or lesson learned.  </div>
