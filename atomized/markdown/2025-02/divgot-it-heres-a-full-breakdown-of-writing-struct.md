## Content
<div>Got it. Here’s a full breakdown of writing structures, along with guidance on how to analyze works he’s inspired by and how to embrace exploratory writing.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>1. Study What Inspires You (Reverse Engineering Narrative Structure)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>If he’s working on a comic, graphic novel, or any specific format, he should pick 2-3 examples that inspire him and analyze their structure.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Activity: Break Down the Narrative of Works You Love</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>For each of his chosen inspirations, he should:</div>
<div>  1.  Summarize the entire plot in a paragraph.</div>
<div>  2.  Break the story into key beats (e.g., major events, turning points, character shifts).</div>
<div>  3.  Analyze the pacing—Where does it slow down? Where does it ramp up?</div>
<div>  4.  Identify themes—What’s the core message? What ideas repeat?</div>
<div>  5.  Look at dialogue vs. visuals (for comics/graphic novels)—How much is told through text vs. images?</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Example Breakdown for a Comic or Graphic Novel:</div>
<div>  •  What happens in each panel on the first page?</div>
<div>  •  What introduces conflict or tension?</div>
