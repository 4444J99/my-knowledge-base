<div>Finding Credible Sources</div>
<div>When evaluating online sources, it is important to consider the source's credibility and any bias that may be present.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>To find credible academic sources, look for articles from reputable journals or websites. Another research method is to search for news articles on your topic. These can provide current information and real-world examples to support your argument. However, it is important to be aware of any bias that may be present in these sources.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>When evaluating online sources, there are a few things to remember. First, consider the credentials of the author or website. Is this a reliable source of information? Second, look for any potential bias in the source material. Does the author have a vested interest in promoting one side of the argument over another? For example, is the owner of the &quotGet Fit Treadmill&quot writing an article about the benefits of using a treadmill for overall health over other forms of exercise? The owner would certainly have reason to be biased! Consider how recently the information was published. Is it still relevant to your argument?</div>
