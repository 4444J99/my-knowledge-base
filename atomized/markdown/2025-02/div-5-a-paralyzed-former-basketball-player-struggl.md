<div>Step 2: Rewrite Your Logline Five Different Ways</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Now that you have your logline, rewrite it with these five abstract and exploratory prompts:</div>
<div>  1.  What if the protagonist was their own antagonist? (Example: Instead of an external rival, the biggest conflict is self-doubt.)</div>
<div>  2.  What if this story happened in a completely different era? (Example: What if Whiplash was about a medieval knight being trained by a brutal mentor?)</div>
<div>  3.  Flip the character arc—who would they be if they failed? (Example: What if Musashi never grew, never questioned himself?)</div>
<div>  4.  Change the focus—what happens if the story is told from the antagonist’s POV?</div>
<div>  5.  Abstract theme challenge—rewrite the logline using only emotions or abstract ideas instead of literal plot.</div>
