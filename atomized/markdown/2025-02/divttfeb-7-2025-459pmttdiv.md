<div><tt>In Eudora Welty’s story “A Worn Path” symbolism plays a very important part within the themes of perseverance and sacrifice through the character Phoenix Jackson.  Throughout her journey she embarks on some struggles that stood as barriers in her plight but through perseverance she was able to overcome the challenges she faced in order to make the sacrifice for her grandson. </tt></div>
<div><tt><br></tt></div>
<div><tt>While there are many symbols throughout this short story there are 2 that stand out for me. The Hunter that Pheonix encountered was a white man who had just finish hunting and stood with his dog. The white man questioned Phoenix on her whereabouts, how old she was and from how far she had come. The hunter told Pheonix to go back home before something happen to her. The hunter in this story symbolizes racial tension. Pheonix being an old, small negro woman remained head strong despite the Hunter trying to intimidate and scare her. </tt></div>
<div><tt><br></tt></div>
<div><tt>Another symbol was the path that Pheonix had taken to reach her destination. Along her path she came across thorny bushes, a scarecrow that she thought was a ghost and a log that crossed a creek. Throughout her journey she spoke to herself out loud and didn’t waver. She showed great determination and commitment to her grandson. </tt></div>
