<div>Robert McKee (<i>Story</i>) and Scott McCloud (<i>Understanding Comics</i>) both explore <b>how stories work</b>, but from <b>two fundamentally different perspectives</b>.</div>
<div>  •  <b>McKee focuses on narrative structure</b>—the foundation of conflict, escalation, and character choice that makes a story compelling across any medium.</div>
<div>  •  <b>McCloud focuses on the form of comics</b>—how <b>visual language, panel transitions, and reader participation</b> shape the way a story is told.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>By putting them in conversation with each other, we can explore <b>how storytelling shifts when moving between film and graphic novels</b>—and how Derek can use both approaches to <b>maximize his own work</b>.</div>
