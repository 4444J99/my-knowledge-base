<div><b><i><h2>Understanding Comics</h2></i></b><b><h2>, McCloud: Chapter 1</h2></b></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Scott McCloud’s <i>Understanding Comics</i> is <b>one of the most influential books on visual storytelling</b>, breaking down the <b>unique strengths and mechanics of comics</b> as a medium. Unlike McKee, who focuses on <b>story principles applicable to all mediums</b>, McCloud <b>analyzes how comics use time, space, and visual design</b> to create meaning.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>McCloud defines comics as:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>  <i>“Juxtaposed pictorial and other images in deliberate sequence, intended to convey information and/or produce an aesthetic response in the viewer.”</i> </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>This simple yet profound definition highlights that <b>comics are not just pictures with words—they are a unique form of sequential art that manipulates time, space, and reader interaction.</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>1. Comics Manipulate Time Through Space</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>McCloud’s <b>core argument</b> is that comics use <b>panel composition and layout to control time</b>, something <b>no other medium does in the same way</b>.</div>
