## Content
<div>  •  Exposition – Introduce setting, characters, and stakes.</div>
<div>  •  Rising Action – Introduce conflict, obstacles build tension.</div>
<div>  •  Climax – The peak moment of tension (a major decision, battle, revelation).</div>
<div>  •  Falling Action – Consequences of the climax unfold.</div>
<div>  •  Resolution – Ties up loose ends, establishes the new normal.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>B. Three-Act Structure</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Best for: Films, comics, graphic novels, novels.</div>
<div>  •  Act 1: Setup – Establish the protagonist, world, and inciting incident.</div>
<div>  •  Act 2: Confrontation – The protagonist struggles, faces obstacles, and reaches a low point.</div>
<div>  •  Act 3: Resolution – The final confrontation leads to a conclusion.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>C. The Hero’s Journey (Joseph Campbell)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Best for: Epic quests, fantasy, sci-fi, coming-of-age stories.</div>
<div>  1.  Ordinary World – The hero’s normal life.</div>
<div>  2.  Call to Adventure – Something disrupts their world.</div>
<div>  3.  Refusal of the Call – The hero resists change.</div>
<div>  4.  Meeting the Mentor – Guidance appears.</div>
<div>  5.  Crossing the Threshold – The hero enters the unknown.</div>
