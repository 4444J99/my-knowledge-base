<div>Experimental and “Mythic” Approaches to Symbolic Operating Systems</div>
<div>Finally, we consider more imaginative approaches to organizing and interfacing with a unified data architecture. These range from cutting-edge hypermedia systems to artistic or myth-inspired computing environments. While not all are directly practical, they can spark ideas for MythOS’s design philosophy: • Hypermedia and Transclusion: We touched on Project Xanadu as a vision of a global hypertext repository. Key features of Xanadu’s design included transclusion (inclusion of content by reference) and non-linear navigation. Modern offshoots of these ideas appear in tools like TiddlyWiki, Federated Wiki, and even web APIs – where rather than copying data, you link or embed it live. An experimental OS could use hypermedia as the basis: instead of a file explorer, you have a web of documents where you can zoom in/out, with every piece knowing its origin and connections. For example, MythOS might allow you to click on a paragraph in a note and see every other place that paragraph (or quote) is used (a true two-way link).
