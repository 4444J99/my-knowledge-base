## Content
<div>When reviewing a peer’s draft, please use the following checklist:</div>
<div>  1.  <b>Thesis &amp Organization:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  Is the thesis statement clear and well-defined?</div>
<div>  •  Does the essay follow a logical structure?</div>
<div>  •  Are the main points supported by details?</div>
<div>  2.  <b>Content &amp Clarity:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  Is the essay engaging and does it address the prompt effectively?</div>
<div>  •  Are transitions smooth between paragraphs?</div>
<div>  •  Is there sufficient evidence and explanation for key ideas?</div>
<div>  3.  <b>Grammar &amp Style:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  Are there issues with grammar, punctuation, or spelling?</div>
<div>  •  Is the tone appropriate for the audience?</div>
<div>  •  Does the sentence structure vary appropriately?</div>
<div>  4.  <b>APA/Formatting (if applicable):</b><br></div>
<div>  •  Are in-text citations and the reference page formatted correctly?</div>
<div>  •  Is there consistent use of APA style?</div>
<div>  5.  <b>Overall Feedback:</b><br></div>
<div>  •  What is one strength of this draft?</div>
<div>  •  What is one area for improvement?</div>
<div>  •  Any additional suggestions or questions?</div>
