## Content
<div>Prewriting is the part of the writing process where you generate ideas for writing. Writing thoughts on paper or typing phrases in a Word document primes your brain for writing. Many different prewriting strategies work well. </div>
<div>Freewriting</div>
<div>Freewriting is just what it sounds like—you write freely whatever comes into your head. The idea here is to keep your fingers moving. Time yourself for 5 minutes, and keep writing even if you make a typo or if you're writing, &quotI don't know what to write.&quot The uninterrupted flow allows the writer to tap into their subconscious during this exercise. Set the freewriting aside and do another freewriting after a few minutes. Often, you can identify recurring ideas when you do a series of four or five freewritings. A recurring notion might be fruitful to develop into a more extended piece of writing. </div>
<div>Asking Questions</div>
<div>Asking questions can help you both identify a topic for writing and identify ideas about that topic. For example, you may want to ask and answer questions such as &quotWhat am I interested in?&quot or &quotWhat news article/issue has captured my interest recently?&quot to identify a writing topic.  </div>
