## Content
<div>Here are some great guidelines when you are starting a free-write warm-up exercise:</div>
<ol>
<li>Keep your fingers moving on the keyboard for 5 minutes. </li>
<li>Write everything that comes to mind, even seemingly unrelated comments like, &quotI hate writing. This is too hard. I don't think I would like eating squid if I had the chance.&quot</li>
<li>Don't self-edit. Get it all down without worrying about how it looks or whether or not it is correct.</li>
<li>Be outrageous. Use vocabulary and descriptions that sound overboard, silly, or absurd. Make comparisons and connections to other subjects (even if they seem at first glance to be irrelevant or unrelated).</li>
<li>Keep writing, no matter what, until your first 5 minutes are up, and then stop.</li>
</ol>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>Instructions</b></div>
<div>Perform the following tasks:</div>
<div>On a Word document or the text entry box:</div>
<div>Think about writing. What do you feel when you think about writing an essay? What do you imagine when you think about writing an essay? Has there ever been a piece of written language that has influenced you in some way? What was it, and why? Do you think something you write could ever affect someone else similarly?</div>
