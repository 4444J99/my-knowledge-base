## Content
<div>Fiction creates distance from reality, allowing me to try and assume a more objective view on the self (both myself, and the other selves—the collective conscious).  If I can presuppose that in the core of life runs the same thread, then by exploring this thread, and the strands within the threads, then I will be feeling and playing with strings that tie what ever it may be that ties this whole mess together.  To look in the mirror is hard, but to looking the mirror with the face of another man or woman becomes less so. </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I must write in opposition to—or against—capital, insincerity, and boundaries.  The art must come before the paycheck—the paycheck in most senses being nothing but a deterrent to the greatness behind the hands.  </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I must write in support of freedom of speech and expression.  Nothing is off limits in art, and it is through exploration of both the serene and the macabre that there can be bridged an understanding in such a polar reality.  To make someone else feel a thing they were in concrete opposition to, and create sympathy and understanding for—that is what must be supported.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I need to learn how to not allow depression, and the need to feel good through unhealthy stimulation, control my life.  Dependency sits on top of every priority and stares down the totem, pulling my decisions along in such a way to be made satiated.  The hard question: where is the line?  I wonder if as an addict, can I still participate in activities that I am not inclined to be addicted to and still maintain a fruitful environment for creativity.  </div>
