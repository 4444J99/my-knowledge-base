<div>II. Proust Questionnaire Icebreaker (20 minutes)</div>
<div>  1.  Purpose</div>
<div>  •  A fun and engaging activity to introduce students to self-reflection and deeper thinking about their writing identities.</div>
<div>  2.  Instructions</div>
<div>  •  Answer questions that explore values, aspirations, and perspectives on life and writing.</div>
<div>  •  Encourage open participation and sharing.</div>
<div>  3.  Transition</div>
<div>  •  Discuss how personal reflection informs writing style and voice.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>III. Lecture: The Importance of Grammar, Punctuation, and Spelling (45 minutes)</div>
<div>  1.  Grammar</div>
<div>  •  The system and structure of a language, including syntax (sentence structure), morphology (word formation), and phonology (sound system).</div>
<div>  •  Proper grammar ensures clarity, coherence, and professionalism.</div>
<div>a. Discussion Questions:</div>
<div>  •  Concrete: Can you think of a time when grammar confusion led to a misunderstanding? What grammar rules do you find most challenging?</div>
<div>  •  Abstract: Does grammar reflect intelligence? Can breaking grammar rules ever be justified?</div>
