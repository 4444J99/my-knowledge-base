<div>Optimization:</div>
<div>  •  Use incremental builds to speed up CI/CD.</div>
<div>  •  Profile performance on low-end devices (e.g., iPhone 8, budget Androids).</div>
<div>  •  Conduct accessibility audits early using axe and VoiceOver/TalkBack.</div>
<div>Challenges to Mitigate:</div>
<div>  •  Prioritize core gameplay (3D board, basic rules) to avoid scope creep.</div>
<div>  •  Schedule regular playtests to refine UX and performance.</div>
<div>Key Considerations for Mobile</div>
<div>  1  Performance: Optimize for mid-tier devices (e.g., 2GB RAM, quad-core CPU). Use LOD, texture compression, and lightweight shaders. Test on devices like iPhone SE (2020) and Samsung Galaxy A51.</div>
<div>  2  Storage: Keep initial app size under 200 MB, with eras downloaded on-demand via Addressables. Offer an “Offline Mode” with one era pre-installed.</div>
<div>  3  Battery: Minimize background processes (e.g., disable AI inference when idle). Provide a low-power mode reducing effects and frame rate.</div>
<div>  4  Retention: Hook players with quick onboarding (e.g., 1-minute Roman Villa tutorial). Use push notifications for multiplayer moves and daily challenges.</div>
<div>  5  Monetization: Consider a freemium model with free access to one era and in-app purchases for additional eras or cosmetics. Avoid pay-to-win mechanics to maintain fairness.</div>
