<div>  6  Robust Toolchain: The detailed pipeline (Blender, three.js/Unity, WebXR, Firebase) and CI/CD integration shows a practical approach to development, balancing creative ambition with technical rigor.</div>
<div>Potential Challenges</div>
<div>  1  Performance on Mobile: The 3D-first approach using three.js or Unity, combined with dynamic lighting, physics, and AR, risks performance issues on mid-to-low-end mobile devices. High-fidelity assets (PBR materials, dynamic day/night cycles) could strain memory and battery life.</div>
<div>  2  Complexity of UX: The touch-centric gestures (one-finger orbit, two-finger pinch zoom/pan) and AR overlay may overwhelm casual players, especially those unfamiliar with backgammon. The learning curve for both gameplay and navigation could deter retention.</div>
<div>  3  AR Integration: While WebXR/ARKit/ARCore is exciting, AR adoption on mobile is inconsistent due to hardware limitations and user familiarity. Ensuring a seamless fallback to non-AR mode is critical.</div>
<div>  4  AI and Backend Load: The Archivist AI’s real-time hints, Socratic dialogs, and cinematic replays, combined with backend persistence for multiplayer and profiles, could introduce latency or high server costs, especially with on-device AI inference.</div>
