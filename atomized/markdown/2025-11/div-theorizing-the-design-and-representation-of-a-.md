<div> Theorizing the Design and Representation of a Rhetorical-Linguistic Operating System</div>
<div>Executive Summary</div>
<div>This report presents a comprehensive theoretical framework for the design and representation of a Rhetorical-Linguistic Operating System (RLOS), designated under the project name RE:GE_OS_RHETORICAL_LINGUISTICS. Conceived as a modular, AI-driven ecosystem, the RLOS integrates analysis, generation, and visualization of human language across phonological, morphological, syntactic, semantic, pragmatic, discourse, sociolinguistic, and iconographic layers. It operates in two modes: RAW, which permits unfiltered research outputs for exploratory purposes, and PUBLIC, which enforces safety gates to mitigate risks such as misinformation or cultural insensitivity. The system is inherently multilingual, supporting automatic detection and processing in languages including Arabic (ar), Chinese (zh), English (en), Spanish (es), Hindi (hi), Japanese (ja), and Russian (ru), while accommodating bidirectional text, symbolic systems, visual modalities (e.g., emoji, hieroglyphics, icons, typography), and multimodal elements (e.g., sound and gesture).</div>
<div>Drawing from systems architecture, semiotics, and media theory, this framework positions the RLOS not merely as software but as a semiotic artifact that evolves through feedback loops, embodying rhetorical persuasion, cognitive interpretation, and ethical governance. The design emphasizes cross-layer interoperability, formal unification via mathematical structures, and aesthetic representation to foster both academic inquiry and practical application.</div>
