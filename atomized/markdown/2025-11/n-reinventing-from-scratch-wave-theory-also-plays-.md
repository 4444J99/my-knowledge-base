<div>**Handoff Seal:** The handoff seal layer defines the protocols and artifacts needed to safely transfer context from one agent or session to the next. If continuity is about the long-term narrative, *handoff* is about the immediate next step – making sure nothing is lost between runs and that future AI agents or collaborators can pick up exactly where the previous left off. The term “seal” evokes a metaphorical wax seal on a letter or a *signet* on a scroll: it certifies completeness and integrity at the moment of transfer. Practically, this means every session or workflow ends with a **handoff procedure**: the current state is summarized (in natural language and structured metadata), given a unique ID, and saved to the archive (e.g. a session log with an index entry). This summary is like an executive narrative *and* a diff report, highlighting what was accomplished, what remains open, and any changes to naming schemes or rules. By formalizing a closing ritual, we imbue each session’s end with significance – it’s a **Handoff Seal** that future agents must break (open) to continue the work.
