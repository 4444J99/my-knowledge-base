## Content
<div>$AUTOMATION_ENGINE – the orchestrator that responds to events and runs automations.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Each root is governed by specific AI roles.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>An “Architect” role can modify $OS_ROOT specs and conventions.</div>
<div>An “Archivist” role writes to $ARCHIVE_ROOT and maintains indices.</div>
<div>A “Prompt-Smith” role curates $PROMPT_ROOT.</div>
<div>A “Builder” role operates in $REPO_ROOT, emitting code or structured assets.</div>
<div>A “Conductor” role configures $AUTOMATION_ENGINE to wire events and jobs.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>These roles can be embodied by different AIs, human collaborators, or both; what matters is that their responsibilities are clearly partitioned.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>2.2 $OS_ROOT Structure</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>$OS_ROOT should be treated as the conceptual kernel. At minimum it contains:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/config</div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/specs</div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/worlds</div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/agents</div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/meta</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>$OS_ROOT/config: base configuration, including environment variable templates, global constants, and naming-scheme definitions.</div>
