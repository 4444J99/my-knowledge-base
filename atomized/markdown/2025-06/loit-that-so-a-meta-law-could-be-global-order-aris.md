loit that. So a meta-law could be: Global order arises from local interactions following simple rules. This is optimistic: one doesn’t always need a central planner; complexity can self-arrange (with caveats to avoid unwanted emergent outcomes). It ties into recursion too, since emergence often yields self-similar structures at higher levels.</div>
<div>  •  Human-Centered Patterns: Across philosophy, law, social science, and art, there is an implicit law: human nature. Golden Rule, categorical imperative, human rights – all presuppose empathy and dignity as fundamentals. Maslow’s hierarchy and archetypes presuppose common human needs and psyche structures. Even game design’s “8 kinds of fun” is based on universal psychological pleasures. So one might say a meta-law is universality of certain human traits (people everywhere respond to fairness, get motivated by certain needs, recognize certain story beats). This underlies why cross-cultural myths share motifs, or why smile and laughter are universal. It’s what makes universal declaration of rights conceivable. If we abstract it: despite cultural variation, the human species has common hardware (brain, body) and arguably common software (cognitive biases, emotions), which generates universal patterns in our behavior and creations. Therefore, broad principles (don’t lie, appreciate beauty in symmetry &amp rhythm, fear death, seek meaning) keep recurring. </div>
