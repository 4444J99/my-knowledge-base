## Content
<div>  •  Cause and Effect (Causality): Almost all systems assume a chain of causation. Scientific laws explicitly connect cause and effect (forces produce acceleration, mutations drive evolution). Karma and the Threefold Law apply cause-effect to morality. Chekhov’s gun and narrative structure enforce cause-effect in storytelling (setup leads to payoff). In math, induction is a cause-effect proof step (base causes next link). Even chaos theory, though highlighting unpredictability, is deterministic cause-effect under the hood. The craving for cause-effect can be seen in conspiracy theories or myths when real causality isn’t clear – humans will invent causes to fill gaps (which gave rise to sympathetic magic’s assumed causes). Principle of sufficient reason is a philosophical form of this meta-law: everything that exists must have a reason【50†L120-L127】. In legal systems, cause-effect is mirrored in consistent application of law (action X leads to penalty Y). Causality is such a bedrock that breaking it (like time-travel paradoxes or quantum indeterminacy) deeply challenges our understanding.
