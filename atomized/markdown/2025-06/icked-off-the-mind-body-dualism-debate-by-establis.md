<div>  •  The Hero’s Journey (Monomyth) (Domain: Mythology/Narratology) – A narrative pattern identified by Joseph Campbell that appears across myths and stories worldwide. It describes a hero’s adventure in a cycle of stages: Call to Adventure -&gt Departure from the ordinary world -&gt Trials and Helpers -&gt Abyss/Climax (death and rebirth) -&gt Transformation -&gt Return with boon. This universal narrative template underlies countless tales from Odysseus to Star Wars. Examples: In the myth of Buddha: the prince leaves his palace (Departure), faces temptations and discovers enlightenment under the bodhi tree (Trials and Abyss), and returns to teach (Return with boon). In modern film: The Matrix follows Neo through a call (the red pill), departure into the Matrix, mentorship by Morpheus, an abyss in his death and revival as “the One,” and his return to free others. Implications: The hero’s journey is a structural law of storytelling, tapping into archetypal human experiences of growth. It suggests a deep psychological pattern (some tie it to Jungian archetypes of self-discovery). Symbolically, it represents transformation – the cycle of separation, initiation, and return.
