<div>  •  Entropy and Decay vs. Order and Growth: The second law of thermodynamics introduces entropy – a drift to disorder – which appears metaphorically in many domains. Murphy’s law (“things go wrong”) is a folksy entropy. Organizations tend to become inefficient or oligarchic over time (a social entropy, needing bursts of reform energy to counteract). Stories often move from order to chaos (rising conflict) and then require a restoration of order (resolution). Entropy is balanced by its opposite – in open systems, energy/input can create negentropy (order). Life itself is order maintained by constant energy use, fighting entropy temporarily. Similarly, civilizations rise (creating order in form of culture, law) and fall (to entropy via corruption, overextension). Many spiritual systems acknowledge an age of decline or a need for renewal cycles. In creative work, there’s a tendency for ideas to drift without structure (entropy) unless an editor or structure imposes order. So, a meta-pattern: Systems naturally drift toward disorder unless energy or intelligence is applied to maintain or improve order.
