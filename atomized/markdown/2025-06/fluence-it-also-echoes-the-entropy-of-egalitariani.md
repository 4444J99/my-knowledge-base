<div>  •  Law of Supply and Demand (Domain: Economics) – A fundamental economic principle stating that the price of a good will adjust until quantity supplied by producers equals quantity demanded by consumers, assuming a competitive market. Description: If demand for an item increases (more people want it) and supply remains same, prices tend to rise, attracting more supply and/or rationing demand. Conversely, if supply increases (a flood of goods) and demand stays same, prices fall. Equilibrium price occurs where supply = demand. Examples: If a new gaming console is released in limited quantity (low supply) but many want it (high demand), the price in secondary markets skyrockets. Over time, as production catches up (supply rises), the price comes down to normal. In labor markets, if there’s high demand for a certain skill but few qualified workers, wages for that job go up (and more people train for that skill, increasing future supply). Implications: Supply and demand act like natural laws in market dynamics – often depicted by the intersection of supply and demand curves. It introduces the concept of equilibrium in social science.
