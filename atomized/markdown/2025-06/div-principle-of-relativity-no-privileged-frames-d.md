## Content
<div>  •  Principle of Relativity (No Privileged Frames) (Domain: Physics) – The laws of physics are the same in any inertial frame (constant velocity frame of reference). There is no absolute “still” frame of reference; motion is relative. Description: “There is no way to say which inertial observer is ‘really’ moving and which is ‘really’ standing still. One observer’s state of rest is another’s state of uniform motion… no experiment can deem either point of view correct or incorrect”【33†L555-L559】. This insight, implicit in Newton’s first law and later formalized by Einstein, means physics must be invariant under changes of inertial frame. Examples: Whether you’re on a smoothly flying airplane or standing on the ground, the basic physics (ignoring turbulence or acceleration) works the same – you can’t tell who is “moving” without an external reference. Implications: This principle underlies both special relativity and the idea of fairness in measurement – it highlights equivalence of perspectives, connecting to a broader theme of relativity in knowledge and observation.</div>
<div>  •  Law of Conservation of Energy (Domain: Physics) – Energy cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; it can only change form【24†L434-L439】. The total energy remains constant. Examples: A roller coaster at the top of a hill has gravitational potential energy that converts to kinetic energy as it descends. The sum total (ignoring friction) stays the same. If friction is included, energy isn’t lost but converted to heat. Implications: This is a fundamental law of continuity and bookkeeping in the universe. Symbolically, it suggests that things persist in one form or another. In spiritual or philosophical terms, it’s akin to the idea that a soul or essence transforms rather than vanishes – though science confines this to measurable energy.</div>
