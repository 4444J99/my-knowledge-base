Storywise, they provided consistency (internal law) for Asimov’s universe and a framework for conflict (many stories show unforeseen loopholes or conflicts among the laws). The three laws are a cultural touchstone – even actual roboticists discuss them, though they’re not practical to implement literally with today’s tech. They illustrate the principle of embedding constraints into a system to govern behavior. Cross-domain, it’s analogous to constitutional laws for governments or rules of engagement for soldiers – trying to ensure ethical behavior via codified rules. Asimov’s laws also hint at hierarchy of values (human safety &gt obedience &gt self-preservation), much like human ethical systems prioritize certain principles over others. They foreshadow real debates in AI ethics: ensuring AI can’t harm humans is goal #1, but then how to balance autonomy and obedience, etc. In worldbuilding terms, they show how setting a few clear laws can enrich storytelling by creating expectations and conflicts when those expectations are met or subverted.</div>
