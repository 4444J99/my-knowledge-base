<div>  •  Magical Law of Similarity &amp Contagion (Domain: Sympathetic Magic/Anthropology) – Described by anthropologist James Frazer: principles underlying much of folk magic. Law of Similarity: like affects like – a similarity between two things can be used to influence one via the other (thus homeopathic magic). Law of Contagion: once two things have been in contact, they continue to influence each other at a distance (thus contagious magic). Examples: Similarity – voodoo dolls or poppets: a doll resembling a person is treated as the person; harm to the doll (which looks similar) is believed to harm the person. In folk remedies, sometimes a plant that resembles an organ (Doctrine of Signatures) is used to treat ailments of that organ (e.g., walnut resembling a brain, used for mental health). Contagion – using a person’s hair or nail clippings in a spell aims to affect them, because those items were once part of their body (thus “contaminated” with their essence). Another example: believers may keep a relic of a saint, thinking the saint’s virtue continues to reside in it and bless the holder.
