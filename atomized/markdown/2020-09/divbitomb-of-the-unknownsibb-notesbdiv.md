<div>Your email is structured well: the first and last paragraphs speak to your ethos. The first highlights your achievements and the last lets my ego know that you’ve researched me. One formatting note: the center paragraph of block text in the email is very dense and hard to digest. I am not saying I don’t understand it, but from the perspective of an agent/publisher receiving tons of emails, breaking it up into smaller bite size pieces will be more visually appealing and less of roadblock to getting someone who doesn’t care to actually read it and grasp the ideas your presenting.<b><br></b></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>The three quotes that open the collection do what they’re intended to: provide some meta-commentary and offer insights into the thematic content of the work as a whole; they do not feel cheaply used or as placeholders for the heavy lifting you display in the proper contexts of the work. Ending on the quote that is a question allows that thought to hover over the coming proceedings.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Available in Standard Sizes — The Second Person does wonders in this first piece. It assumes the direct address tone of the Wandering Recruiter speaking to a teen…however, I’m in the head of the teen and everything the Recruiter promised in his sales pitch is beginning to feel fake. You also achieve a POV that feels timeless, it’s looking to the future and to the past simultaneously: “I know how this is gonna end.” </div>
