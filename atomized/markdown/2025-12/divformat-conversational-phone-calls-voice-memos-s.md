<div>Option A: Adapt a Classical Text</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Pick something you’ve always wanted to unpack: Greek tragedy, Shakespeare, a contemporary classic that matters to you. We redesign it so audience input reshapes which themes dominate, how characters are perceived, what emotional valence the piece carries overall. The classical structure becomes the skeleton; audience agency becomes living tissue. Example: Sophocles’ Antigone. Audience votes collectively influence how much emphasis falls on state authority vs. individual conscience. Creon’s intransigence isn’t predetermined; it’s shaped by what the audience collectively finds most dramatically compelling. The play’s outcome (tragedy or redemption) shifts based on distributed input, but the narrative coherence remains intact because the parameter space is carefully bounded.</div>
