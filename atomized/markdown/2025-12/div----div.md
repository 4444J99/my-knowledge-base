## Content
<div>----</div>
<div>Of course. Re-imagining &quotInfinite Wiki&quot as a tool for a personal knowledge base is a fascinating idea. Instead of a public-facing encyclopedia, it would become a dynamic, self-organizing &quotsecond brain&quot that helps you connect and explore your own thoughts.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Let's call this theoretical application &quotSynapse&quot.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Here’s how the core functionalities would be adapted for a personal knowledge base:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Core Concept: From Knowledge Consumer to Idea Gardener</div>
<div>The fundamental shift is from consuming general knowledge to cultivating personal insights. Synapse wouldn't just define terms; it would weave together your own notes, ideas, and documents into an interconnected web. The AI's role changes from being a &quotgenerator of facts&quot to a &quotserendipity engine&quot and an &quotintellectual partner.&quot</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Adapting the Key Features</div>
<div>Data Source: Your Personal Corpus</div>
<div>Instead of calling out to the open internet for every definition, Synapse would be grounded in your personal data. This could be a local folder of Markdown files, a connection to a service like Obsidian or Notion, or its own internal database of your notes. This becomes the &quotsource of truth.&quot</div>
