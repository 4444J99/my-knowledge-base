<div>Her eyes were open, but she couldn't move, not just yet. The anger from yesterday hadn't found its proper place in her limbs to warrant any meaningful movement. Instead, she laid, blankets tight around her throat, her feet sticking out of the bottom of the crooked blanket, sock less. She laid: she thought and she thought and she thought and she thought. Collecting yesterday's aggravations, neatly organizing them on the shelf, placing them in order of impending size. It was important to decide which specific aggravation would yank open her tightly wound lips and force the first burst of harsh volumes that once released would continue to resonate in each corner of each room, building, then subsiding only once her eyes were closed once again. </div>
