## Content
<div>FAU MFA Writer’s Room</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I’m writing you all to invite you to participate in a collaborative project with me over the next few months. My intention is to write the first season of a television show. I hesitate to call it a television show from the get-go for while it will be written like a television show would be written, the immediate ambition of the project would be to create a serialized audio/podcast, or a sound play.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>One major element I feel the MFA is missing is the ability to create and work in a collaborative space—and since the highest paid job for a writer is in television (a writer’s room (the room in which the showrunner and the writers of a television series collaborate on the plot and develop the script))—I feel as though getting a taste for that type of environment would be extraordinarily beneficial. </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I know that the idea of writing with others, and not having ‘final cut,’ can be scary/overwhelming/unwelcomed—but whenever again in our life can we insure that so much untapped talent would be in the same place with the similar goals and aspirations at the same time.</div>
