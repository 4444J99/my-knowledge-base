<div>Thebes was destroyed by a band of heroes from Argos under the leadership of Polynices, brother of Eteocles and one of the sons of Oedipus. his effort failed; but the sons of these heroes (collectively called the <i>epigoni</i> were later successful in capturing Thebes; and some of them subsequently fought at Troy as well. So by the genealogical relationship between these generations of heros, a chronological relationship is established between the Theban and the Trojan cycles. Ovid adopts this relationship, developing the Theban material in the earlier part of the poem, and the Trojan meterial in the later books.</div>
