<div>Student Success Statement</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>My nearly seven years of teaching experience instructing students in one of the most diverse areas in the country reflects the polar opposite of my upbringing in New York City. Until working on my graduate degree, I often found myself in rooms surrounded by folks who looked similar to me. However, the time spent instructing at various schools in South Florida shifted that role; suddenly, I was the minority in the classroom. I found, however, that nothing seemed to change!</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>My goal in the classroom is to foster both diversity and equity of thought. All ideas are welcome, discussed, and celebrated with kindness and understanding. I meet all of my students at their individual educational levels and evaluate their work on merit.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>While working at Florida Atlantic University's Center for Excellence in Writing, I learned that spending twenty minute sessions with individual students on their own specific work would greatly benefit their retaining of the work done over the course of the session. The writing center held both in-person consultations and online consultations via Skype. Before each major assignment, I would create a block of time where students would virtually meet with me over Skype (or something similar) and discuss not only their work, but also about the class as a whole. These bi-monthly meetings also help to keep students accountable for their own work, and it puts a face and explanation to an instructor's feedback while also welcoming questions and an ongoing dialogue.</div>
